# Digital Services Enhancement Audit Work Program
## Digital Services Security Assessment

> Per BNM RMiT Nov 2025 — Paragraphs 16.4 and 16.5, mapped to Appendix 7 Part D

### Engagement Overview
- **Engagement Type:** Digital Services Enhancement IESP Assessment
- **Regulatory Basis:** Paragraphs 16.4, 16.5
- **Scope Clauses:** Appendix 7 Part D (items 1a, 2a-e)
- **Frequency:** Prior to launch of new or materially enhanced digital services
- **Reporting:** Appendix 7 Part D

---

### Phase 1: Planning and Scoping

| Step | Activity | Deliverable |
|------|----------|-------------|
| P-01 | Obtain a description of the digital service being launched or enhanced — scope, functionality, target users, channels (web, mobile app, API), and underlying technology stack | Digital service specification |
| P-02 | Identify the threat model for the digital service — what are the key risks based on the service type (e.g., online banking, mobile payments, digital onboarding)? | Threat model or risk profile |
| P-03 | Obtain the security architecture and design documents for the digital service | Security architecture pack |
| P-04 | Identify integration points — upstream and downstream systems, third-party services, API connections | Integration point mapping |
| P-05 | Obtain the FI's security testing reports (penetration test, vulnerability assessment, code review) for the digital service | Security testing reports |
| P-06 | Review applicable regulatory requirements and map to Part D control areas | Regulatory mapping matrix |

---

### Phase 2: Detailed Testing

#### Domain 1: Access Control (Part D item 1a + item 2a-c)

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| DS-01 | Verify customer authentication mechanisms meet security requirements | 1. Review the authentication mechanisms for the digital service (password, OTP, biometric, device binding)<br>2. Verify multi-factor authentication (MFA) is enforced for login and high-risk transactions<br>3. Check password/credential policy: minimum complexity, history, expiry, lockout thresholds<br>4. Verify authentication tokens/sessions are securely managed (secure cookies, token expiry, binding to device/IP) | - Authentication design document<br>- MFA configuration<br>- Password/credential policy<br>- Session management configuration | Pass: MFA enforced for login and high-risk transactions, credential policy meets industry standards, and session management is secure. Fail: No MFA, weak credential policy, or insecure session management |
| DS-02 | Verify customer identity verification for digital onboarding (if applicable) | 1. Review the digital identity verification process (eKYC)<br>2. Verify identity document validation mechanisms (OCR, NFC chip reading, database verification)<br>3. Check liveness detection for biometric verification<br>4. Verify that identity verification results are logged and auditable<br>5. Assess fraud detection controls during onboarding (device fingerprinting, velocity checks) | - eKYC process flow<br>- Identity verification tool configuration<br>- Liveness detection configuration<br>- Audit log samples<br>- Fraud detection rules | Pass: Robust identity verification with document validation, liveness detection, auditable records, and fraud detection controls. Fail: Weak identity verification, no liveness detection, or no fraud controls |
| DS-03 | Verify authorisation and role-based access controls | 1. Review the authorisation model for the digital service (RBAC, ABAC)<br>2. Verify that access to functions and data is based on the user's role and entitlements<br>3. Check that privilege escalation is prevented — can a user access another user's data or functions?<br>4. Verify API authorisation — are API endpoints protected against unauthorised access?<br>5. Test for insecure direct object references (IDOR) in the API layer | - Authorisation model design<br>- Role-to-function mapping<br>- API authorisation configuration<br>- Penetration test results (IDOR testing) | Pass: RBAC/ABAC enforced, privilege escalation not possible, API endpoints authorised, and no IDOR vulnerabilities found. Fail: Weak authorisation, privilege escalation possible, or IDOR vulnerabilities present |
| DS-04 | Verify session management security | 1. Review session management implementation (session ID generation, token binding, timeout, concurrent session handling)<br>2. Verify session tokens are cryptographically random and of sufficient length<br>3. Check that sessions expire after a defined idle period and absolute period<br>4. Verify that session is invalidated on logout, password change, and MFA re-challenge<br>5. Check concurrent session controls — can the user be logged in from multiple devices simultaneously? Is this controlled? | - Session management configuration<br>- Token generation mechanism<br>- Session timeout settings<br>- Concurrent session policy | Pass: Cryptographically random tokens, idle and absolute timeouts enforced, sessions invalidated on security events, and concurrent sessions controlled per policy. Fail: Predictable tokens, no timeouts, sessions persist after security events, or uncontrolled concurrent sessions |
| DS-05 | Verify account lockout and brute-force protection | 1. Review account lockout policy (threshold, duration, reset mechanism)<br>2. Verify rate limiting is implemented for authentication endpoints<br>3. Check CAPTCHA or equivalent bot protection for login and registration<br>4. Verify that lockout notifications are sent to the customer<br>5. Test that lockout cannot be bypassed through different channels or endpoints | - Lockout policy configuration<br>- Rate limiting configuration<br>- CAPTCHA/bot protection<br>- Notification configuration<br>- Bypass testing results | Pass: Account lockout after defined threshold, rate limiting enforced, bot protection in place, customer notified of lockouts, and no bypass possible. Fail: No lockout, no rate limiting, or lockout can be bypassed |

#### Domain 2: Online Transaction Security (Part D item 2)

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| DS-06 | Verify transaction authentication and authorisation controls | 1. Review the transaction authentication mechanism (transaction signing, OTP, biometric confirmation, TAC)<br>2. Verify that high-risk transactions require step-up authentication<br>3. Check that transaction details are presented to the customer for confirmation before execution<br>4. Verify that the authentication is bound to the specific transaction (not reusable)<br>5. Assess protection against man-in-the-middle and man-in-the-browser attacks | - Transaction authentication design<br>- Step-up authentication configuration<br>- Transaction confirmation flow<br>- Transaction binding mechanism<br>- MitM/MitB protection measures | Pass: Transaction-specific authentication with step-up for high-risk transactions, confirmation presented to user, authentication bound to transaction, and MitM protections in place. Fail: No transaction authentication, authentication is reusable, or no MitM protections |
| DS-07 | Verify transaction limits and fraud monitoring | 1. Obtain default and configurable transaction limits (daily, per-transaction, cumulative)<br>2. Verify that limits are enforced at the application and backend levels<br>3. Review fraud monitoring rules for the digital service (anomaly detection, velocity checks, geolocation, device fingerprinting)<br>4. Check that suspicious transactions trigger alerts and/or blocks<br>5. Verify that customers can manage their own limits within defined bounds | - Transaction limit configuration<br>- Fraud monitoring rules<br>- Sample fraud alert records<br>- Customer limit management interface | Pass: Transaction limits enforced at both application and backend, fraud monitoring with anomaly detection in place, suspicious transactions trigger alerts/blocks. Fail: No limits, limits enforced only at frontend, or no fraud monitoring |
| DS-08 | Verify end-to-end encryption for sensitive transactions | 1. Verify TLS 1.2 or higher is enforced for all communications (no fallback to lower versions)<br>2. Check certificate configuration (valid, trusted CA, correct hostname, HSTS enabled)<br>3. Verify that sensitive data in transit is not exposed in URLs, logs, or error messages<br>4. Check for certificate pinning in mobile applications (if applicable)<br>5. Verify API endpoint encryption and mutual TLS for B2B integrations | - TLS configuration (SSL Labs test or equivalent)<br>- Certificate details<br>- HSTS configuration<br>- Log review for sensitive data exposure<br>- Certificate pinning configuration<br>- mTLS configuration for APIs | Pass: TLS 1.2+ enforced, valid certificates with HSTS, no sensitive data in logs/URLs, and certificate pinning for mobile apps. Fail: Weak TLS, invalid/self-signed certificates, sensitive data exposure, or no HSTS |
| DS-09 | Verify secure API design and implementation | 1. Obtain API documentation and review API design against OWASP API Security Top 10<br>2. Verify API authentication (OAuth 2.0, API keys, mTLS as appropriate)<br>3. Check rate limiting and throttling on API endpoints<br>4. Verify input validation and output encoding<br>5. Check that API versioning and deprecation are managed<br>6. Review API gateway configuration for security enforcement | - API documentation<br>- OWASP API Security assessment results<br>- API authentication configuration<br>- Rate limiting configuration<br>- API gateway configuration | Pass: API design addresses OWASP API Top 10, authentication is robust, rate limiting enforced, input validation in place, and API gateway enforces security policies. Fail: OWASP API Top 10 vulnerabilities present, no rate limiting, or no input validation |
| DS-10 | Verify non-repudiation for critical transactions | 1. Identify critical transaction types that require non-repudiation (fund transfers, contract signing, mandate changes)<br>2. Verify that transaction records capture: who, what, when, from where, and how the transaction was authenticated<br>3. Check that transaction records are tamper-proof (append-only, digitally signed, or blockchain-anchored)<br>4. Verify audit trail retention meets regulatory requirements | - Non-repudiation design<br>- Transaction record format and content<br>- Tamper-proofing mechanism<br>- Audit trail retention configuration | Pass: Critical transactions have complete audit trails capturing all required elements, records are tamper-proof, and retention meets requirements. Fail: Incomplete audit trails, tamperable records, or insufficient retention |

#### Domain 3: Mobile Device Security (Part D item 2e)

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| DS-11 | Verify mobile application security controls | 1. Review the mobile application security architecture<br>2. Verify that the app detects rooted/jailbroken devices and responds appropriately (warning, restricted functionality, or block)<br>3. Check that sensitive data is not stored in plaintext on the device (use keychain/keystore)<br>4. Verify code obfuscation and anti-tampering mechanisms<br>5. Verify that the app does not expose sensitive data in application logs, screenshots, or clipboard | - Mobile security architecture<br>- Root/jailbreak detection configuration<br>- Local data storage review<br>- Obfuscation/anti-tampering evidence<br>- Data leakage assessment | Pass: Root/jailbreak detection active, sensitive data secured in keychain/keystore, code obfuscated, anti-tampering in place, and no data leakage through logs/screenshots. Fail: No root detection, plaintext data on device, no obfuscation, or data leakage found |
| DS-12 | Verify mobile application distribution and update management | 1. Verify the app is distributed only through official app stores (Apple App Store, Google Play)<br>2. Check that the app enforces minimum version requirements (force update for critical security patches)<br>3. Verify app signing and integrity verification<br>4. Check that push notification content does not include sensitive information<br>5. Review mobile device management (MDM) or mobile application management (MAM) requirements for corporate users | - App store distribution evidence<br>- Minimum version enforcement<br>- App signing configuration<br>- Push notification content policy<br>- MDM/MAM policy (if applicable) | Pass: Official app store distribution only, forced updates for critical patches, app integrity verified, and push notifications do not leak sensitive data. Fail: Side-loading permitted, no forced updates, or sensitive data in push notifications |
| DS-13 | Verify mobile-specific threat protections | 1. Check for runtime application self-protection (RASP) or equivalent runtime protections<br>2. Verify that the app detects and responds to debugging attempts, emulator execution, and hooking frameworks (Frida, Xposed)<br>3. Check that secure communication channels cannot be intercepted by proxy tools without detection<br>4. Verify device binding/registration mechanisms | - RASP configuration<br>- Anti-debugging/emulator detection<br>- Proxy/MitM detection<br>- Device binding mechanism | Pass: Runtime protections deployed, anti-debugging/emulator detection active, proxy detection in place, and device binding implemented. Fail: No runtime protections, no anti-debugging, or no proxy detection |

#### Domain 4: Data Integrity (Part D item 2d)

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| DS-14 | Verify input validation and data integrity controls | 1. Review input validation implementation (server-side validation is mandatory, client-side is supplementary)<br>2. Verify validation covers: data type, length, range, format, and allowed characters<br>3. Test for common injection attacks: SQL injection, XSS, command injection, LDAP injection<br>4. Verify output encoding is applied to prevent XSS<br>5. Check that file upload functionality (if any) validates file type, size, and content | - Input validation design<br>- Server-side validation configuration<br>- Penetration test results (injection testing)<br>- Output encoding implementation<br>- File upload validation configuration | Pass: Server-side validation for all inputs, no injection vulnerabilities found in testing, output encoding applied, and file uploads validated. Fail: Client-side only validation, injection vulnerabilities present, or no output encoding |
| DS-15 | Verify data integrity during processing and storage | 1. Review data integrity controls in the transaction processing chain<br>2. Verify checksums or hash verification for data in transit between system components<br>3. Check database integrity controls (constraints, referential integrity, transaction logging)<br>4. Verify reconciliation processes between the digital channel and core systems<br>5. Confirm that data integrity violations trigger alerts | - Data integrity control design<br>- Checksum/hash implementation<br>- Database integrity configuration<br>- Reconciliation procedures and reports<br>- Alert configuration for integrity violations | Pass: Data integrity controls at each processing stage, checksums for inter-component data, database integrity enforced, reconciliation performed, and violations trigger alerts. Fail: No integrity controls, no reconciliation, or violations not detected |
| DS-16 | Verify protection against data manipulation and replay attacks | 1. Verify anti-replay mechanisms (nonce, timestamp, sequence number) for critical transactions<br>2. Check that transaction requests cannot be intercepted and modified (integrity protection in transit)<br>3. Verify that duplicate transaction detection is in place<br>4. Test for parameter tampering vulnerabilities in the application | - Anti-replay mechanism design<br>- Transit integrity protection<br>- Duplicate detection configuration<br>- Parameter tampering test results | Pass: Anti-replay mechanisms in place, transaction integrity protected in transit, duplicate detection active, and no parameter tampering vulnerabilities. Fail: No anti-replay protection, transaction manipulation possible, or parameter tampering found |

#### Supplementary: Application Security Testing Verification

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| DS-17 | Verify comprehensive security testing was performed prior to launch | 1. Obtain the security testing scope and plan<br>2. Verify that the following tests were performed: SAST (static analysis), DAST (dynamic analysis), penetration testing (web and mobile), API security testing, and infrastructure vulnerability assessment<br>3. Review test results and finding severity distribution<br>4. Verify that all high and critical findings are remediated before launch<br>5. Confirm testing was performed by qualified testers (internal security team or reputable third party) | - Security testing plan<br>- SAST report<br>- DAST report<br>- Penetration test report<br>- API security test report<br>- VA report<br>- Remediation evidence for high/critical findings<br>- Tester qualifications | Pass: All required security tests performed, high/critical findings remediated, and testing performed by qualified testers. Fail: Missing test types, unremediated high/critical findings, or unqualified testers |
| DS-18 | Verify secure software development lifecycle (SSDLC) practices | 1. Review the FI's SSDLC for the digital service<br>2. Verify that security requirements are defined at the design phase<br>3. Check that threat modelling was performed for the service<br>4. Verify secure coding standards are applied and enforced (e.g., through code review or automated scanning)<br>5. Confirm that security sign-off is required before production release | - SSDLC documentation<br>- Security requirements specification<br>- Threat model<br>- Secure coding standards<br>- Code review/scanning records<br>- Security sign-off records | Pass: SSDLC is followed with security requirements, threat modelling, secure coding enforcement, and security sign-off before release. Fail: No SSDLC, no threat modelling, or no security sign-off |
| DS-19 | Verify privacy and data protection controls for the digital service | 1. Review privacy impact assessment (PIA) for the digital service<br>2. Verify that personal data collection is limited to what is necessary (data minimisation)<br>3. Check that consent mechanisms are implemented where required<br>4. Verify data retention and deletion policies are enforced for the digital service<br>5. Confirm privacy notices are clear and accessible to users | - Privacy impact assessment<br>- Data collection inventory<br>- Consent mechanism implementation<br>- Data retention/deletion configuration<br>- Privacy notice review | Pass: PIA completed, data minimisation practiced, consent mechanisms in place, retention policies enforced, and privacy notices are clear. Fail: No PIA, excessive data collection, no consent mechanism, or unclear privacy notices |
| DS-20 | Verify business continuity and availability for the digital service | 1. Review the availability targets for the digital service (e.g., 99.9% uptime)<br>2. Verify that the service architecture supports high availability (load balancing, auto-scaling, failover)<br>3. Check monitoring and alerting for service availability<br>4. Verify that a disaster recovery plan covers the digital service<br>5. Review results of the most recent DR test for the service | - Availability targets<br>- HA architecture design<br>- Monitoring and alerting configuration<br>- DR plan (digital service section)<br>- DR test results | Pass: Availability targets defined, HA architecture in place, monitoring active, and DR tested within 12 months. Fail: No availability targets, no HA design, or DR untested |

### Phase 3: Reporting

| Step | Activity | Deliverable |
|------|----------|-------------|
| R-01 | Consolidate all test results and assign risk ratings (High/Medium/Low) to each finding | Findings register with risk ratings |
| R-02 | Map findings to Appendix 7 Part D control items for structured reporting | Findings-to-Part D mapping |
| R-03 | Provide a clear overall assessment: is the digital service fit for launch from a security perspective? | Overall security readiness assessment |
| R-04 | Draft recommendations with specific remediation steps, responsible parties, and timelines — distinguish between must-fix-before-launch and post-launch improvements | Recommendations tracker (pre-launch vs. post-launch) |
| R-05 | Conduct findings walkthrough with FI digital, security, and risk teams | Walkthrough meeting minutes |
| R-06 | Prepare the formal Digital Services Enhancement report in Appendix 7 Part D format | Final report |
| R-07 | Obtain FI management sign-off on the report and management action plans | Signed report and action plans |
