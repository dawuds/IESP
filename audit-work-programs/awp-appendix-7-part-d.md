# Appendix 7 Part D — Minimum Controls Assessment
## Audit Work Program

> Per BNM RMiT Nov 2025 — Appendix 7 Part D, applicable to ALL IESP engagements under Paragraph 14.1 and 17.1

### Engagement Overview
- **Engagement Type:** Universal — applies to ALL IESP engagements (DCRA, NRA, Cloud, Digital Services, Emerging Tech)
- **Regulatory Basis:** Appendix 7 Part D
- **Scope:** Item 1 (Security Requirements — 6 areas) and Item 2 (Online Transactions and Services — 5 sub-areas)
- **Frequency:** Assessed in every IESP engagement cycle
- **Reporting:** Appendix 7 Part D negative attestation (embedded within the engagement-specific Appendix 7 Part A report)

> **Note:** Part D is the universal baseline. Every IESP engagement must include an assessment of Part D minimum controls regardless of the engagement type. The IESP must provide a negative attestation confirming whether the FI has met, or has not met, each minimum control. Findings against Part D controls are reported alongside engagement-specific findings in the Appendix 7 Part A report.

#### Item 1(a): Access Control

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| PD-01 | Verify the FI has implemented access control policies and mechanisms across technology assets | 1. Obtain the FI's access control policy and supporting standards<br>2. Verify policy covers user registration, de-registration, access provisioning, and privilege management<br>3. Sample 20 user accounts — verify access rights are consistent with job roles (least privilege principle)<br>4. Check that access reviews are performed periodically (at least quarterly for privileged accounts, semi-annually for standard users)<br>5. | PD-02 | Verify privileged access management controls | 1. Obtain the privileged access management (PAM) policy or standard<br>2. Obtain the list of all privileged accounts (system admin, DBA, root, domain admin)<br>3. Check that privileged access is time-bound and subject to approval workflow<br>5. Review privileged session logs for the past 3 months for anomalies | - PAM policy<br>- Privileged account inventory<br>- PAM tool configuration and logs<br>- Session recording samples | Pass: Privileged accounts are inventoried, individually assigned or vault-managed, time-bound where feasible, and session activity is logged and reviewable. ### Phase 3: Item 2 — Online Transaction and Services Controls

**Regulatory reference:** Appendix 7 Part D, Item 2(a) through 2(e)

> **Applicability note:** Item 2 applies to FIs that offer online transaction services and/or mobile-based financial services. If the FI does not offer such services, the IESP must document the basis for exclusion in the Part D applicability matrix (see P-05).

#### Item 2(a): Customer Identity Authentication

| Ref | Test Objective | Test Steps | Expected Evidence | Pass/Fail Criteria |
|-----|---------------|------------|-------------------|-------------------|
| PD-13 | Verify controls to prevent session takeover and man-in-the-middle (MITM) attacks | 1. Review the online service architecture for session management controls<br>2. Confirm session tokens are cryptographically random, have appropriate expiry, and are invalidated on logout<br>4. Check for anti-session-fixation controls (new session ID on authentication)<br>5. Verify certificate pinning for mobile applications (where applicable) | - TLS configuration scan results (e.g., SSL Labs)<br>- Session management configuration<br>- Application security assessment results<br>- Mobile app certificate pinning evidence | Pass: TLS 1.2+ enforced with strong cipher suites, HSTS enabled, session tokens are cryptographically secure with proper lifecycle management, and anti-session-fixation controls are in place. Fail: Weak TLS, no HSTS, predictable session tokens, or missing anti-fixation controls |
| PD-14 | Verify internal controls to prevent compromise of systems, applications, and databases supporting online services | 1. Verify application hardening — check that unnecessary services, default accounts, and debug modes are disabled in production<br>2. Fail: Unhardened systems, stale or no security testing, unprotected databases, or no segmentation |
| PD-15 | Verify multi-level authentication and real-time verification mechanisms | 1. Review the FI's authentication architecture for online services — identify authentication factors used (knowledge, possession, inherence)<br>2. Verify multi-factor authentication (MFA) is implemented for customer login and high-risk transactions<br>3. Check for out-of-band authentication mechanisms (e.g., OTP via SMS/push, hardware token)<br>4. Verify real-time verification controls for high-value or unusual transactions<br>5. Assess the strength and appropriateness of authentication methods relative to transaction risk | - Authentication architecture documentation<br>- MFA configuration evidence<br>- OTP/out-of-band mechanism design<br>- Transaction risk-based authentication rules | Pass: MFA is implemented, out-of-band mechanisms are used for high-risk transactions, and authentication strength is proportionate to transaction risk. Fail: Single-factor authentication for sensitive operations, no out-of-band mechanism, or authentication not risk-calibrated |
| PD-16 | Verify secure session handling and authentication database security | 1. Verify session timeout is enforced (idle timeout and absolute timeout)<br>2. Review authentication failure handling — account lockout after defined failed attempts with secure unlock process | - Session management configuration<br>- Password hashing algorithm evidence<br>- Authentication database access controls<br>- Account lockout configuration | Pass: Session timeouts enforced, credentials stored with strong hashing and salting, authentication database is access-restricted and encrypted, and account lockout is configured. | PD-17 | Verify strong cryptographic implementation | 1. Review the FI's cryptographic standards — verify use of recognised algorithms (AES-256, RSA-2048+, ECDSA P-256+)<br>2. Verify key management practices — key generation, storage (HSM or equivalent), rotation schedule, and destruction<br>3. Check that deprecated algorithms (DES, 3DES, RC4, MD5, SHA-1) are not used for security functions<br>4. Verify cryptographic implementation has been reviewed or tested (e.g., by penetration test or code review) | - Cryptographic standards document<br>- Key management procedures<br>- HSM configuration and inventory<br>- Crypto assessment results | Pass: Recognised algorithms with adequate key strength are used, key management follows industry practices (NIST SP 800-57 or equivalent), no deprecated algorithms in use, and implementation has been assessed. Fail: Weak or deprecated algorithms, poor key management, no HSM for critical keys, or unassessed implementation |

### Phase 4: Reporting and Negative Attestation

| Step | Activity | Deliverable |
|------|----------|-------------|
| R-01 | Consolidate all Part D test results (PD-01 through PD-29) and assign pass/fail status to each minimum control | Part D minimum controls assessment matrix |
| R-02 | For each failed control, assign risk rating (High/Medium/Low) and draft specific remediation recommendations with timelines | Part D findings register with risk ratings and recommendations |
| R-03 | Prepare the Part D negative attestation statement — confirm whether the FI has met, or has not met, each minimum control with reference to specific findings | Draft negative attestation (Part D section of Appendix 7 Part A report) |
| R-04 | Conduct findings walkthrough with FI management to validate accuracy and obtain management responses | Walkthrough meeting minutes |
| R-05 | Integrate Part D findings and negative attestation into the parent engagement report (Appendix 7 Part A) | Final Appendix 7 Part A report with embedded Part D attestation |
| R-06 | Obtain FI management sign-off on Part D findings and management action plans | Signed management action plan for Part D findings |
