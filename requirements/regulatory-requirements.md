# IESP Regulatory Requirements — Comprehensive Reference

> BNM RMiT Nov 2025 — All clauses triggering or relating to Independent External Service Provider reviews

## Purpose

This document consolidates every RMIT clause that creates an obligation, expectation, or guidance relating to IESP engagements. It is the authoritative internal reference for the compliance and attestation team to determine what the regulation actually requires.

---

## 1. Paragraph 1.4 — BNM Power to Require Independent External Review

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 1.4 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | BNM may require a financial institution to engage an independent external party to conduct a review of the FI's technology risk management practices where the Bank identifies material weaknesses in the FI's technology risk management. |
| **IESP Relevance** | This is a catch-all power. Even where no specific trigger event (DCRA, NRA, cloud, etc.) applies, BNM can direct an IESP engagement at its discretion. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) The FI must comply promptly. (2) The scope is determined by BNM based on the identified weaknesses. (3) The FI bears the cost. (4) Results must be reported to BNM. |
| **Practical Note** | This clause is typically invoked after supervisory examinations, incident escalations, or thematic reviews reveal systemic control failures. |

## 2. Paragraph 9.3(c) — Independent Views on Third-Party Assessments

| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 9.3(c) |
| **Marker** | G (guidance) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | The board risk committee or equivalent should obtain independent views on technology risk assessments, including assessments involving third-party service providers, to ensure objectivity and comprehensiveness. |
| **IESP Relevance** | While not a direct IESP trigger, this establishes the governance expectation that third-party risk assessments should be subject to independent challenge. This supports the rationale for engaging IESPs for DCRA, NRA, and cloud reviews. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Board-level oversight of technology risk assessments. (2) Independent challenge function should cover third-party assessments. (3) Frequency aligned with risk appetite. |

## 3. Paragraphs 10.46–10.49 — Third Party Service Provider Management

### 10.46 — Third Party Risk Assessment
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.46 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution must conduct a comprehensive risk assessment before entering into any arrangement with a third party service provider for technology-related services. The assessment must consider operational, security, legal, regulatory, and concentration risks. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Sets the foundation for cloud and third-party assessments. The risk assessment produced here feeds into the IESP scope for cloud pre-implementation reviews (17.1). |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Risk assessment before engagement. (2) Must cover confidentiality, integrity, availability. (3) Must assess provider's security posture, BCP, incident management. (4) Must assess regulatory compliance implications. |

### 10.47 — Contractual Requirements
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.47 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Contractual arrangements with third-party service providers must include provisions for audit access, security requirements, incident notification, data protection, business continuity, and exit strategy. |
| **IESP Relevance** | IESPs assessing third-party arrangements must verify these contractual provisions are in place. Relevant to DCRA (third-party DC), cloud reviews, and NRA (managed network services). |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Right to audit (including by BNM). (2) Security requirements and SLAs. (3) Incident notification obligations. (4) Data handling and destruction requirements. (5) Exit and transition provisions. |

### 10.48 — Ongoing Monitoring of Third Parties
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.48 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Financial institutions must establish robust ongoing monitoring mechanisms for third-party service providers, including periodic assessments of service delivery, security, and compliance. |
| **IESP Relevance** | The IESP should assess whether the FI has established effective ongoing monitoring. This is a key control tested in cloud subsequent reviews (17.2) and DCRA (third-party DC). |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Regular performance monitoring. (2) Periodic security assessments of third parties. (3) Escalation procedures for service failures. (4) Review of third-party incident reports. |

### 10.49 — Concentration Risk
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.49 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Financial institutions must manage concentration risk arising from reliance on a single or limited number of third-party service providers, including cloud service providers. |
| **IESP Relevance** | IESPs conducting cloud pre-implementation reviews must assess concentration risk as part of Appendix 10 scope. This includes assessing the FI's exit strategy and ability to migrate to alternative providers. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Concentration risk assessment. (2) Exit strategy and portability considerations. (3) Alternative provider identification. (4) Impact assessment for provider failure. |

## 4. Paragraphs 10.50–10.52 — Cloud Services

### 10.50 — Cloud Risk Assessment
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.50 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution intending to adopt cloud services must conduct a comprehensive risk assessment covering technology, operational, legal, and regulatory risks specific to cloud computing, taking into consideration the risks outlined in Appendix 10. |
| **IESP Relevance** | This is the foundational risk assessment that precedes the IESP engagement for cloud pre-implementation review under 17.1. The IESP must verify this assessment was properly conducted. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Risk assessment must be conducted before cloud adoption. (2) Must address risks in Appendix 10. (3) Must consider data sovereignty, jurisdictional, and regulatory requirements. (4) Must assess impact on existing risk profile. |

### 10.51 — Cloud Security Controls
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.51 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Financial institutions must implement appropriate security controls for cloud services, including encryption, access management, network security, and monitoring. |
| **IESP Relevance** | The IESP must assess the adequacy of cloud security controls as part of the pre-implementation review. These controls form part of Appendix 10 Part B assessment scope. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Encryption of data in transit and at rest. (2) Strong access management and authentication. (3) Network security and segmentation. (4) Continuous monitoring and logging. (5) Incident detection and response. |

### 10.52 — Cloud Governance
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 10.52 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Financial institutions must establish a cloud governance framework addressing roles, responsibilities, policies, and oversight mechanisms for cloud service adoption and operations. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Cloud governance is assessed under Appendix 10 Part A. The IESP must evaluate the maturity and effectiveness of the governance framework. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Cloud strategy aligned with business strategy. (2) Defined roles and responsibilities. (3) Cloud-specific policies and procedures. (4) Board/senior management oversight. (5) Skills and competency management. |

## 5. Paragraphs 13.1–13.4 — Technology Audits

### 13.1 — Technology Audit Function
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 13.1 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution must ensure that its technology audit function has the competency, resources, and independence to provide effective assurance over technology risk management. |
| **IESP Relevance** | The technology audit function is distinct from IESP reviews but serves a complementary role. Where the internal audit function lacks specific competency (e.g., cloud, emerging tech), external specialists may supplement. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Adequate competency and staffing. (2) Independence from first and second lines. (3) Comprehensive audit universe covering technology. (4) Risk-based audit planning. |

### 13.2 — Scope of Technology Audits
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 13.2 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Technology audits must cover data centres, network infrastructure, security operations, access management, change management, incident management, business continuity, and third-party service provider management. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Technology audit coverage overlaps with IESP scope. Where DCRA/NRA is due, the technology audit plan should coordinate with the IESP engagement to avoid duplication and ensure complementary coverage. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Comprehensive coverage of technology domains. (2) Inclusion of third-party service provider assessments. (3) Coverage of cloud and emerging technology where applicable. (4) Risk-based prioritization. |

### 13.3 — Audit Competency
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 13.3 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Technology auditors must possess relevant qualifications, certifications, and experience to effectively assess technology risks and controls. |
| **IESP Relevance** | This standard also applies to IESPs by analogy. The FI must ensure IESPs engaged for DCRA, NRA, cloud, or emerging tech reviews have equivalent or higher competency. This is reinforced by Appendix 7 Part C. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Relevant professional certifications (e.g., CISA, CISSP, CCSP). (2) Technical competency in the subject matter. (3) Understanding of regulatory requirements. (4) Industry experience. |

### 13.4 — Outsourced Audit
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 13.4 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Where technology audit functions are partially or fully outsourced, the financial institution remains responsible for the quality and coverage of audit work. The outsourced provider must meet the same independence and competency standards. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Establishes that outsourcing audit does not transfer accountability. The same principle applies to IESP engagements — the FI retains responsibility for ensuring IESP quality and for acting on findings. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) FI retains accountability. (2) Outsourced auditors must meet independence requirements. (3) Quality oversight by internal audit leadership. (4) Clear terms of reference. |

## 6. Paragraphs 14.1–14.2 — External Party Assurance

### 14.1 — Data Centre Resilience Assessment (DCRA)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 14.1 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution must engage a technically competent independent external service provider to assess the resilience of its data centre at least once every three years or upon any material change to the data centre infrastructure, whichever is earlier. The assessment must cover the requirements in paragraphs 10.24 to 10.28. The results must be deliberated by a designated board-level committee. Where the financial institution relies on a third-party data centre, it may rely on independent third-party assurance reports, provided the assurance is consistent with the FI's risk appetite and considers similar risks. |
| **IESP Relevance** | **Primary IESP trigger.** This is one of the two core mandatory IESP engagements. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Technically competent IESP. (2) 3-year cycle or material change. (3) Scope: 10.24–10.28. (4) Board-level committee deliberation. (5) Third-party DC reliance conditions. (6) Report per Appendix 7 Part A. |
| **Practical Checklist** | See `/requirements/dcra-requirements.md` for detailed work program. |

### 14.2 — Network Resilience Assessment (NRA)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 14.2 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution must engage a technically competent independent external service provider to assess the resilience of its network infrastructure at least once every three years or upon any material change to the network design, whichever is earlier. The assessment must cover the requirements in paragraphs 10.36 to 10.43. The results must be deliberated by a designated board-level committee. |
| **IESP Relevance** | **Primary IESP trigger.** This is the second core mandatory IESP engagement. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Technically competent IESP. (2) 3-year cycle or material change. (3) Scope: 10.36–10.43. (4) Board-level committee deliberation. (5) Report per Appendix 7 Part A. |
| **Practical Checklist** | See `/requirements/nra-requirements.md` for detailed work program. |

## 7. Paragraphs 16.4–16.5 — Digital Services Enhancement Assurance

### 16.4 — Notification for Technology-Related Applications
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 16.4 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution must notify BNM prior to launching or materially enhancing digital services. The notification must be accompanied by: (a) an independent assessment of the technology risks and security controls by a competent external service provider, in the format prescribed in Appendix 7 Part A; (b) confirmation from senior management on the adequacy of controls. |
| **IESP Relevance** | **Direct IESP trigger** for digital services that do not qualify for simplified notification under 16.3. The IESP must assess technology risks and security controls per Part D of Appendix 7. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) IESP assessment before BNM notification. (2) Assessment per Appendix 7 Part A format. (3) Controls assessed per Appendix 7 Part D. (4) Senior management confirmation. |

### 16.5 — Assessor Requirements for Digital Services
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 16.5 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | The external service provider engaged under paragraph 16.4 must be competent with a good track record and must comply with the requirements set out in Part C and Part D of Appendix 7. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Establishes the quality and competency bar for IESPs conducting digital services reviews. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Demonstrated competence. (2) Good track record (no adverse regulatory findings). (3) Compliance with Part C (ESP requirements). (4) Assessment of controls in Part D (minimum control domains). |

## 8. Paragraphs 17.1–17.5 — Cloud and Emerging Technology

### 17.1 — Pre-Implementation Consultation (Cloud/Emerging Tech for Critical Systems)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 17.1 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | A financial institution intending to adopt public cloud services or emerging technology for critical systems for the first time must consult BNM before implementation. The consultation submission must include: (a) a comprehensive risk assessment per paragraph 10.50 and Appendix 10, in the format prescribed in Appendix 7 Part A; (b) confirmation from the CISO or board chair in the format prescribed in Appendix 7 Part B; (c) a pre-implementation review by an independent external service provider. For higher-risk services involving customer information processing/storage or cross-border data transmission, the submission must also comply with Part C of Appendix 7. |
| **IESP Relevance** | **Primary IESP trigger for cloud and emerging tech.** The third-party pre-implementation review is a mandatory prerequisite for BNM consultation. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Risk assessment per 10.50 + Appendix 10. (2) CISO/board confirmation per App 7 Part B. (3) IESP pre-implementation review. (4) Higher-risk services: Part C compliance. (5) BNM consultation before adoption. (6) Submission format: App 7 Parts A, B, C, D. |
| **Practical Checklist** | See `/requirements/cloud-pre-implementation.md` and `/requirements/emerging-tech-review.md`. |

### 17.2 — Subsequent Cloud/Emerging Tech Notifications
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 17.2 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | After the initial consultation under 17.1 is completed with no concerns raised by BNM, the financial institution may adopt a notification-based approach for subsequent adoption of public cloud or emerging technology for critical systems, provided: (a) the 17.1 consultation was completed satisfactorily; (b) the TRMF has been enhanced to address cloud/emerging tech risks; (c) independent assurance has been established on the adequacy of the enhanced framework; (d) sufficient incident response plans are in place. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Condition (c) requires independent assurance on the enhanced TRMF, which may involve an IESP. The notification itself must still include the same documentation as 17.1. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Prior 17.1 completed satisfactorily. (2) Enhanced TRMF. (3) Independent assurance on TRMF. (4) Incident response plans. (5) Notification includes App 7 documentation. |

### 17.3 — Exemption for Non-Material Enhancements
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 17.3 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Paragraphs 17.1 and 17.2 do not apply to enhancements to existing cloud or emerging technology implementations that do not materially alter the risk profile previously assessed. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Establishes when an IESP is NOT required. The FI must document the rationale for determining that an enhancement is non-material. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Enhancement must not materially alter risk profile. (2) FI must document materiality determination. (3) Previous assessment must still be current and valid. |

### 17.4 — BNM Discretionary Direction (Cloud/Emerging Tech)
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 17.4 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | BNM may direct a financial institution to conduct additional assessments or engage an independent external service provider, including for non-critical systems, where BNM considers it necessary to address technology risks arising from cloud or emerging technology adoption. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Extends IESP requirements beyond critical systems at BNM's discretion. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) BNM may direct at any time. (2) May cover non-critical systems. (3) FI must comply as directed. (4) Scope determined by BNM. |

### 17.5 — Transition Provisions
| Attribute | Detail |
|-----------|--------|
| **Clause** | 17.5 |
| **Marker** | S (mandatory) |
| **Verbatim Summary** | Financial institutions with existing cloud or emerging technology implementations at the effective date of this policy must comply with paragraphs 17.1 to 17.4 within the transitional period specified by BNM. |
| **IESP Relevance** | Existing cloud deployments that have not undergone 17.1 consultation must do so within the transition period, which will trigger IESP requirements. |
| **Key Requirements** | (1) Inventory existing cloud/emerging tech deployments. (2) Prioritize critical systems for 17.1 consultation. (3) Engage IESP within transition timeline. |

## 9. Appendix 7 — Risk Assessment Report and ESP Requirements

### Part A — Risk Assessment Report Format

| Section | Content |
|---------|---------|
| **Section 1** | Financial institution details (name, license type, contact person) |
| **Section 2** | External service provider details (firm name, lead assessor, qualifications, contact) |
| **Section 3** | Application/system details (description, architecture, data classification, criticality) |
| **Section 4** | Technology risk assessment findings (risks identified, control gaps, severity ratings, recommendations) |
| **Section 5** | Quality assurance (peer review, methodology, limitations, caveats) |
| **Section 6** | Authorised signatory (lead assessor certification, date, firm stamp) |

**IESP Relevance:** This is the mandatory output format for all IESP engagements — DCRA, NRA, cloud, emerging tech, and digital services reviews.

**Key Requirements for Practitioners:**
- Section 4 must use a consistent risk rating methodology (e.g., High/Medium/Low or quantitative)
- Findings must be specific, actionable, and traceable to RMIT clauses
- Quality assurance (Section 5) must document the review methodology and any scope limitations
- The report must be signed by the lead assessor with professional qualifications stated

### Part B — Format of Confirmation

A 9-point attestation that must be signed by the CISO, board committee chair, or designated senior management. The attestation confirms:

1. The FI has conducted a comprehensive risk assessment
2. Identified risks have been assessed and mitigated to acceptable levels
3. Security controls are adequate and effective
4. The FI has competent personnel to manage the technology/service
5. Incident response and BCP arrangements are adequate
6. Contractual arrangements with third parties are adequate
7. Regulatory requirements (including data protection) have been addressed
8. The board/designated committee has been briefed and is satisfied
9. The FI will notify BNM of any material changes

**IESP Relevance:** The IESP does not sign Part B, but the IESP's findings directly inform whether the CISO/board can make these attestations. Significant IESP findings may prevent the attestation from being signed.

### Part C — Requirements on External Party Assurance

Six requirements the IESP must comply with when conducting the review:

| # | Requirement | Detail |
|---|-------------|--------|
| 1 | **Independence** | The ESP must be independent of the FI's technology operations and the systems/services being assessed. No conflicts of interest. |
| 2 | **Competence** | The ESP must have relevant professional qualifications, certifications, and experience in the subject matter. |
| 3 | **Scope** | The ESP must assess all areas specified in the relevant appendix (Appendix 7 Part D, Appendix 10, or Appendix 9 as applicable). |
| 4 | **Methodology** | The ESP must use a systematic, risk-based assessment methodology with clear criteria for evaluating controls. |
| 5 | **Reporting** | The ESP must report findings in the format prescribed in Part A, with clear risk ratings and actionable recommendations. |
| 6 | **Quality Assurance** | The ESP must have internal quality assurance processes, including peer review of assessment work. |

**IESP Relevance:** These are the professional standards the IESP firm must meet. The FI should verify compliance before engagement and document this verification.

### Part D — Minimum Controls to be Assessed

Control domains the IESP must assess:

| Domain | Key Areas |
|--------|-----------|
| **Access Control** | User access management, privileged access, authentication, access reviews |
| **Physical Security** | Data centre physical access, environmental controls, visitor management |
| **Operations Security** | Change management, capacity management, malware protection, backup, logging |
| **Communications Security** | Network security, data transmission security, wireless security, DNS security |
| **Incident Management** | Incident detection, response, escalation, forensics, reporting |
| **Business Continuity** | BCP/DRP, recovery objectives, testing, alternate processing |
| **Online Transaction Controls** | Transaction authentication, session management, fraud detection (for digital services) |
| **Mobile Application Controls** | Mobile app security, device management, secure development (for mobile digital services) |

**IESP Relevance:** This is the minimum scope. The IESP may expand scope based on risk assessment but must not reduce below these domains.

## 10. Appendix 8 — IT and Cyber Risks Associated with Third Party Service Providers

| Area | Key Risks | IESP Assessment Focus |
|------|-----------|----------------------|
| **Governance** | Inadequate oversight, unclear roles, no exit strategy | Verify governance framework, roles, escalation paths |
| **Access Control** | Excessive access, shared credentials, no MFA | Assess access provisioning, reviews, privileged access management |
| **Data Protection** | Data leakage, inadequate encryption, cross-border risks | Verify encryption (transit/rest), data classification, DLP controls |
| **Network Security** | Flat networks, no segmentation, inadequate monitoring | Assess network architecture, segmentation, IDS/IPS, log monitoring |
| **Incident Management** | Delayed notification, no forensics capability, poor coordination | Verify SLAs, notification procedures, joint incident response plans |
| **Business Continuity** | Single points of failure, untested DRP, inadequate RTO/RPO | Assess BCP/DRP adequacy, testing frequency, recovery capabilities |
| **Concentration Risk** | Over-reliance on single provider, no exit strategy | Assess provider diversification, exit planning, data portability |

**IESP Relevance:** Appendix 8 provides the risk taxonomy for third-party assessments. IESPs conducting DCRA (third-party DC), cloud reviews, or NRA (managed services) must address these risk areas.

## 11. Appendix 9 — Guidance on Emerging Technologies

| Area | Key Considerations | IESP Assessment Focus |
|------|-------------------|----------------------|
| **Governance** | Board-approved strategy, risk appetite for emerging tech, skills assessment | Verify governance framework, board awareness, competency plans |
| **Risk Assessment** | Unique risks of emerging tech (bias, explainability, model risk), operational risks | Assess risk identification methodology, risk registers, mitigation plans |
| **Data Management** | Data quality, data lineage, training data governance, privacy | Verify data governance framework, quality controls, privacy impact assessments |
| **Model Risk** | Model validation, performance monitoring, drift detection, retraining | Assess model risk management framework, validation processes, monitoring |
| **Security** | Adversarial attacks, prompt injection, model poisoning, API security | Verify security controls specific to the emerging technology |
| **Testing** | Functional testing, adversarial testing, bias testing, performance testing | Assess testing methodology, test coverage, acceptance criteria |
| **Production Readiness** | Monitoring, incident response, rollback capability, human oversight | Verify production deployment controls, monitoring, fallback mechanisms |
| **Ethics and Fairness** | Bias detection, fairness metrics, transparency, explainability | Assess ethical AI framework, bias testing, explainability mechanisms |

**IESP Relevance:** Appendix 9 is the assessment scope for emerging technology pre-implementation reviews under 17.1. The IESP must evaluate the FI's readiness across all these areas.

See `/requirements/emerging-tech-review.md` for detailed practitioner guidance.

## 12. Appendix 10 — Key Risks and Control Measures for Cloud Services

### Part A — Cloud Governance (7 Areas)

| # | Area | Key Requirements |
|---|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Cloud Strategy** | Alignment with business strategy, board approval, cloud-first vs cloud-selective approach |
| 2 | **Roles and Responsibilities** | Clear RACI for cloud operations, security, compliance, vendor management |
| 3 | **Policies and Procedures** | Cloud-specific policies covering security, data management, access, incident response |
| 4 | **Risk Management** | Cloud risk register, risk assessment methodology, risk acceptance criteria |
| 5 | **Competency** | Cloud skills assessment, training plan, certification requirements |
| 6 | **Compliance** | Regulatory mapping, data residency requirements, cross-border considerations |
| 7 | **Oversight** | Monitoring framework, reporting to board, KRIs/KPIs for cloud services |

### Part B — Cloud Design and Control (14 Areas)

| # | Area | Key Requirements |
|---|------|-----------------|
| 1 | **Architecture Design** | Reference architecture, security zones, multi-tenancy controls |
| 2 | **Identity and Access Management** | IAM policies, privileged access, MFA, federation, service accounts |
| 3 | **Data Protection** | Encryption (transit/rest/use), key management, data classification, DLP |
| 4 | **Network Security** | Virtual network design, segmentation, firewall rules, DNS security |
| 5 | **Compute Security** | Hardening, patch management, container security, serverless security |
| 6 | **Storage Security** | Bucket/blob policies, access logging, encryption, lifecycle management |
| 7 | **Logging and Monitoring** | Centralized logging, SIEM integration, alerting, log retention |
| 8 | **Vulnerability Management** | Scanning, patching, penetration testing, remediation SLAs |
| 9 | **Change Management** | IaC governance, pipeline security, approval workflows, rollback |
| 10 | **Incident Response** | Cloud-specific IR procedures, CSP coordination, forensics in cloud |
| 11 | **Business Continuity** | Multi-region/AZ design, backup, DR testing, RTO/RPO for cloud services |
| 12 | **Vendor Management** | CSP assessment, shared responsibility model, SLA monitoring |
| 13 | **Exit Strategy** | Data portability, migration planning, contract termination provisions |
| 14 | **Regulatory Compliance** | Data residency, audit access, regulatory reporting, BNM access rights |

**IESP Relevance:** Appendix 10 is the primary assessment scope for cloud pre-implementation reviews under 17.1. The IESP must systematically assess all 7 governance areas and all 14 design/control areas.

See `/requirements/cloud-pre-implementation.md` for detailed practitioner guidance.
