This management representation letter is provided in connection with your assessment of Bank Perdana Berhad's migration of the Temenos T24/Transact R23 core banking system to Amazon Web Services (AWS) ap-southeast-1 (Singapore) region, performed pursuant to the Risk Management in Technology (RMIT) Policy Document (November 2025) issued by Bank Negara Malaysia (BNM).

### General Representations

1. **Responsibility for controls.** We acknowledge that the design, implementation, and maintenance of effective technology risk management controls relating to the Temenos T24 cloud migration and ongoing operations is the responsibility of the management of Bank Perdana Berhad. Your assessment does not relieve us of this responsibility.

2. **Completeness of information.** We have provided you with all information, documentation, and access to systems, premises, and personnel that you have requested and that we consider relevant to the scope of your assessment of the T24 cloud environment on AWS. We are not aware of any material information that has been withheld.

3. **Accuracy of information.** To the best of our knowledge, the information, documentation, and explanations provided to you — including architecture diagrams, security configurations, IAM policies, and CloudTrail logs for the AWS ap-southeast-1 environment — are complete, accurate, and not misleading. Where estimates or judgements have been made, these have been made on a reasonable basis.

4. **Disclosure of matters.** We have disclosed to you all matters that we consider may be relevant to your assessment, including but not limited to:
   - All known instances of non-compliance with the RMIT or other applicable regulations relating to technology risk management in the context of the T24 cloud migration
   - All known security incidents, breaches, or near-misses affecting the AWS cloud environment that occurred during or after the assessment period, including three P3/P4 incidents recorded between July and December 2025
   - All known material weaknesses in internal controls relating to the T24 cloud infrastructure, application layer, and supporting services
   - All known fraud or suspected fraud involving management or employees with significant roles in the T24 cloud migration programme
   - All prior audit or assessment findings (internal and external) relating to the T24 system and the cloud migration programme, including the internal audit report dated August 2025

5. **Currency of documentation.** The policies, procedures, standards, and other documentation provided to you are the current, approved versions in effect during the assessment period. ### Technology-Specific Representations

6. **System inventory.** The system and infrastructure inventory provided to you is complete and accurate as at 15 January 2026. The T24/Transact R23 deployment on AWS comprises EC2 instances (application tier), RDS for Oracle (database tier), S3 (object storage), and supporting services including CloudWatch, GuardDuty, AWS KMS, CloudHSM, and AWS Backup. There are no material systems, applications, or infrastructure components within the scope of your assessment that have not been disclosed.

7. **Architecture documentation.** The architecture diagrams, network diagrams, and system documentation provided to you — including the T24 logical architecture diagram v1.4 and the multi-AZ VPC design — accurately represent the current state of the cloud environment within the scope of your assessment.

8. **Configuration information.** The configuration information, settings, and parameters provided to you or made available for your review — including security group rules, NACL configurations, IAM policies, KMS key policies, and CloudTrail settings — accurately reflect the current production environment in AWS ap-southeast-1.

9. **Access controls.** The access control lists, IAM role/policy exports, user access matrices, and privileged access inventories provided to you are complete and current as at 15 January 2026. There are eight root/administrative accounts and 34 custom IAM roles configured for the T24 cloud environment.

10. **Change management.** All material changes to the T24 cloud infrastructure and application during the assessment period have been disclosed and were subject to Bank Perdana Berhad's change management process. Forty-seven change tickets were raised for the cloud environment between October and December 2025, all of which followed the approved change management procedure.

11. **Incident history.** We have disclosed all technology incidents, security breaches, and service disruptions affecting the T24 cloud environment that occurred during the past twelve months (January 2025 to January 2026). Three incidents were recorded, all classified as P3 or P4 severity, with no material impact on data integrity or customer service availability.

12. **Third-party arrangements.** We have disclosed all material third-party and outsourcing arrangements relating to the T24 cloud environment, including the AWS Enterprise Agreement (executed June 2025), the Temenos software licensing and support agreement, and the managed services arrangement with the Bank's systems integrator. The AWS sub-processor list has been provided.

13. **Regulatory matters.** We have disclosed all regulatory observations, directions, or enforcement actions by BNM or other regulators relating to the T24 system, the cloud migration programme, and the broader technology risk management framework. There are no outstanding regulatory directions specific to this migration.

### Representations Specific to Cloud Services Assessment

20. **Cloud service arrangements.** We have disclosed all cloud service arrangements relating to the T24 migration and operations. The deployment uses AWS IaaS services in the ap-southeast-1 (Singapore) region. The following AWS services are utilised: Amazon EC2, Amazon RDS for Oracle, Amazon S3, Amazon VPC, AWS Direct Connect, Amazon CloudWatch, Amazon GuardDuty, AWS Security Hub, AWS CloudTrail, AWS KMS, AWS CloudHSM, AWS Backup, AWS WAF, AWS Shield Advanced, Amazon Route 53, AWS Secrets Manager, and AWS Certificate Manager. No additional cloud service providers are used for the T24 workload. No serverless compute (Lambda) or container services (ECS/EKS) are in scope.

21. **CSP assurance reports.** All relevant cloud service provider assurance reports have been provided, including the AWS SOC 2 Type II report for the period April to September 2025 (issued October 2025), the AWS ISO 27001 certificate (valid to June 2027), the AWS ISO 27017 certificate, and the AWS ISO 27018 certificate. The Complementary User Entity Controls (CUECs) from the SOC 2 report are being mapped to Bank Perdana Berhad's control environment; this mapping is approximately 60% complete as at the date of this letter.

22. **Data sovereignty and cross-border matters.** We acknowledge that the hosting of T24 production data in AWS ap-southeast-1 (Singapore) constitutes cross-border hosting of banking data outside Malaysia. This arrangement has been assessed against BNM's requirements, and the relevant cross-border risk assessment has been completed and documented. The data processing agreement with AWS includes provisions for data residency in the Singapore region, and contractual safeguards are in place to prevent data transfer to other jurisdictions without Bank Perdana Berhad's consent.
