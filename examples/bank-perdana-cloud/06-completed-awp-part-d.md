### Negative Attestation Statement

> Pursuant to Bank Negara Malaysia's Risk Management in Technology (RMiT) policy document (November 2025), Appendix 7 Part D, CyberAssure Advisory Sdn Bhd, as the appointed Independent External Service Provider (IESP), hereby provides the following negative attestation:
>
> Bank Perdana Berhad has **substantially met** the minimum controls specified in Appendix 7 Part D, Item 1 (Security Requirements) and Item 2 (Online Transaction and Services Controls), with the exception of the following areas requiring remediation:
>
> 1. **Item 1(a) — Access Control (PD-01, PD-02):** High-rated deficiency. Gaps identified in IAM governance including 4 stale user accounts, overly permissive IAM policies with wildcard actions, delayed access revocation for leavers/transfers, and absence of a dedicated PAM solution for session recording. *Reference: Finding F-001.*
>
> 2. **Item 1(e) — Information Security Incident Management (PD-09):** Medium-rated deficiency. Cloud-specific incident response playbooks are incomplete — no dedicated playbook for CSP outage/failure scenario or cloud account compromise. *Reference: Finding F-007.*
>
> 3. **Item 1(f) — Business Continuity Management (PD-11):** Medium-rated deficiency. BCP/DRP testing for cloud workloads has not included full-stack disaster recovery failover. Only database-layer failover has been tested; application tier, network, and DNS cutover remain untested. *Reference: Finding F-006.*
>
> All other minimum controls assessed under Part D Item 1 and Item 2 were found to be adequately implemented and operating effectively as at the date of this assessment.
